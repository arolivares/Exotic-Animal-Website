About Common Ravens

The common raven or it's scientific name, Corvus Corax is apart of roughly 10 species of heavy-billed dark birds that are larger than crows. The reason crows amd ravens look so similar is due the the fact that they are closely related. Though they have differences, ravens typically have heavier bills and shaggier plumage around the throat compared to that of a crow. A raven's feathers aren't completely black, they actually have a blue or purplish iridescence to them. The Common Raven has been in the Northern Hemisphere for centuries and has accompanied people for centuries. These birds thrive among humans and in other places as well. Ravens can be found in the outskirts of towns, to forests and deep in the mountains and national parks. To know if a raven is near you, you're going to hear a deep gurgling croak from above you, and you'll see a long-tailed black bird flying with its long wings. The Common Raven is very intelligent making them very dangerous predators in the wild. They can work in pairs to raid bird colonies, and even seen waiting in trees as sheep give birth to then attack the newborns.


Habitat

Woodlands, coniferous forests, rocky coastlines are most common areas for common ravens. But, they can also survive in deserts and mountains in the West but are far less common.

Diet

In the wild, the common raven's diet varies based on their habitat and any available food soruces. Since they are omnivores they'll feed on practically anything, from small mammals to nesting birds, eggs and berries. They are also scvengers, they eat carrion from other predators. In captivity their diet consits of crickets, mice, wax worms, different fruits, and a specialized diet of ground beef, hard boiled eggs and different types of commercial dry pellet feed.


Other facts


Family life

Raven's gather in packs to hunt, but they don't usually gather in large flocks. This is because they don't migrate like other birds. They also keep the same mate for life and use the same nest each year.


Population


The global common raven population is more than 16 million. Because of this, they are at the least concern when it comes to endangered species.


Lifespan

In the wild, ravens can live between 10 to 15 years. But in captivity it's possible they can live between 40-50 years.


Size

Ravens usually range from 22-27 inches (56-59cm) in height and between 1-3.5 pounds(.7-1.6kg). And they also have a wingspan of 45.5-46.5 in(1 meter)



Fun facts

Ravens are highly intelligent animals, and have been known to use tools to obtain food. Ravens and other corvid species are subjects of multiple cognitive research studies focusing on their ability to learn and use tools.
